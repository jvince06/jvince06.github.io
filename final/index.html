Accidental Drug Overdoses in Connecticut (2012-2024)

Starting in the 1990s, accidental drug overdoses have become a national and worldwide crisis. The United States experiences the most accidental overdoses (according to https://www.commonwealthfund.org/blog/2025/us-overdose-deaths-remain-higher-other-countries-trend-tracking-and-harm-reduction) with 324 deaths per 1 million people. While many states are putting resources into fighting the war on drugs and Fentanyl, the primary reason for overdoses, Connecticut is an example of a state that hasn't seen promising results compared to other states.
